## Summary

Cross-section data often show that the wealth of the elderly increases with age, suggesting that the [[The Life Cycle Model of Consumption]] should include a [[Bequeathment]] motive for saving. Using a a model of bequests, and a test for a bequest motive. Empirical findings are that in a ten-year panel data set, the elderly dissaved, in contradiction to most cross-section results. The test offers no support for a bequest motive.
